Common traps

  • sensitive = easily affected emotionally
  • sensible = reasonable, practical
  • childish = immature in a negative way
  • childlike = innocent in a positive way

Examples

He is very sensitive to criticism, but usually makes sensible decisions.

Her writing is clear and readable, but his notes are almost illegible.

Quick Patterns

Verb → Noun

develop → development

decide → decision

fail → failure

Adjective → Noun

simple → simplicity

private → privacy

happy → happiness

Noun → Adjective

care → careful / careless

hope → hopeful / hopeless

child → childish / childlike

Model Examples

The situation became very intense, and the emotional intensity was hard to handle.
He did not want to fail, but one careless mistake led to complete failure.
We all hope for the best and try to stay hopeful even in difficult times.
She is usually careful, but sometimes becomes completely careless with details.
He is still rather immature, but this experience may help him become more mature.

Exam Focus

  • Check the part of speech first.
  • Look for negative meaning before choosing a prefix.
  • Watch out for traps like sensitive and sensible.
  • Use context, not memory alone.
  • Build word families, not isolated words.
