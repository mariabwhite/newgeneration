Confucius (551–479 BC) was one of China's greatest philosophers. He believed that society should be based on respect, loyalty and education. His ideas, known as Confucianism, shaped Chinese culture for over 2,000 years. Another philosopher, Laozi, founded Taoism — a philosophy based on living in harmony with nature. Sun Tzu wrote "The Art of War", which is still studied today by military leaders and business people. Ancient China also gave the world four great inventions: paper, printing, gunpowder and the compass.
